Traditional Dominican Recipes

Explore authentic flavors from the Dominican Republic 🇩🇴

Sancocho

Description: A rich and hearty Dominican stew made with a variety of meats and root vegetables. It is often served during special occasions and family gatherings.

Ingredients

  • Chicken or beef
  • Yuca
  • Plantains
  • Corn
  • Garlic, onion, cilantro

Instructions

  1. Boil meat with seasoning for 30 minutes.
  2. Add yuca, plantains, and corn.
  3. Cook for 1 to2 hours until soft.
  4. Add cilantro and serve hot.

La Bandera

Description: The national dish of the Dominican Republic. It represents the Dominican flag with rice, beans, and meat.

Ingredients

  • White rice
  • Red beans
  • Stewed chicken or beef
  • Salad

Instructions

  1. Cook rice until fluffy.
  2. Prepare beans with seasoning.
  3. Cook meat separately.
  4. Serve together with salad.

Pica Pollo

Description: Dominican-style fried chicken, crispy on the outside and juicy inside, usually served with fries and ketchup.

Ingredients

  • Chicken pieces
  • Lemon juice
  • Garlic
  • Oregano
  • Flour
  • Salt & pepper

Instructions

  1. Marinate chicken with lemon, garlic, and seasoning for 1 hour.
  2. Coat in flour.
  3. Fry until golden and crispy.
  4. Serve with fries.

Mangú

Description: Mashed plantains served with eggs, cheese, and salami.

Ingredients

  • Plantains
  • Butter
  • Onions

Instructions

  1. Boil plantains.
  2. Mash them.
  3. Top with sautéed onions.

Pastelón

Description: Dominican plantain lasagna with meat and cheese.

Ingredients

  • Ripe plantains
  • Ground beef
  • Cheese

Instructions

  1. Fry plantains.
  2. Cook meat.
  3. Layer and bake.

Asopao

Description: A thick rice soup with chicken or seafood.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Chicken
  • Vegetables

Instructions

  1. Sauté ingredients.
  2. Add rice and water.
  3. Cook until thick.

Tres Golpes

Description: A classic Dominican breakfast made with mangú, fried cheese, salami, and eggs.

Ingredients

  • Green plantains
  • Eggs
  • Dominican salami
  • Fried cheese
  • Red onions
  • Oil

Instructions

  1. Boil and mash plantains to make mangú.
  2. Fry cheese until golden.
  3. Fry salami slices.
  4. Cook eggs to your preference.
  5. Sauté onions and serve everything together.

Tostones

Description: Crispy twice-fried green plantains, a popular Dominican side dish.

Ingredients

  • Green plantains
  • Salt
  • Oil for frying

Instructions

  1. Peel and slice plantains.
  2. Fry until lightly golden.
  3. Flatten each piece.
  4. Fry again until crispy.
  5. Season with salt and serve.
